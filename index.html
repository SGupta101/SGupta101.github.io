Welcome to the pages of my life.

I'm currently a junior at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology. As an avid journalist and computer scientist, I hope to explore a field that bridges the gap between the two: computational journalism.

My path as a journalist

Before coming to Jefferson, a school so heavily invested in STEM, I didn't think I would be interested in writing. As the years have progressed I've moved from being a staff writer, to team leader, to incoming print editor-in-chief. As a team leader I wrote articles for tjTODAY as well as guided and edited student journalist articles. I also designed and took photos for our print issues. As print editor-in-chief I will lead discussions for lead-editorials and design our print issues.

My path as a computer scientist

A computer scientist since I stepped foot inside of Jefferson, I have taken classes such as Artificial Intelligence and Mobile App Development. I'm a team leader for Coding Lady Colonials, a femenist-empowered school coding club, as well as designed and lead a class for young girls as an NCWIT AspireIT Leader. I've interned at places such as digital advocacy tech company Phone2Action and as a researcher for the GMU ASSIP program. Further pursuing my interest in this field I have won prizes in hackathons Hack-the-Helyx and HackTJ.

How my two paths led me to computational journalism

As I continued on the path through both fields I soon realized I don't have to pick one; it doesn't have to be a choice between STEM or humanities.

Why computational journalism is important to me

Especially in confusing times as these, with COVID-19 and the Black Lives Matter Movement, it's important for journalists to continue reporting while also maintaining trust with their readers. The computer science appeals to logos and ethos aspect through the use of tools such as data and algorithmic verification and allows the readers to get a sense of the bigger picture. The journalism aspect appeals topathos and dives into the details of the story.

What is computational journalism?

Computational journalism is journalism with the incorporation of algorithms to better gather and display data as well as deliver more accurate news without bias. It has the future of unraveling complex stories in a more efficent and understandable way and inspires a trustworthy relationship between the reader and journalist.
